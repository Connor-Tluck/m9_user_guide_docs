# Sign Assembly QA/QC Workflow

<Note>
  Follow these steps to review and approve sign assemblies and their components.
</Note>

<Stepper>
  <StepperItem title="Step 1: Open Dataset">
    Select the dataset (click the icon circled in red).  
    <img src="/images/image58.png" alt="Open dataset icon" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 2: Enable Color Coding">
    Turn on “Color signs based on assemblies” (circled in red).  
    <img src="/images/image59.png" alt="Color signs based on assemblies" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 3: Check Feature Type">
    Once loaded, click the middle button and ensure “Type” is checked.  
    <img src="/images/image60.png" alt="Ensure Type is checked" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 4: Filter Assemblies">
    Click the left button to filter sign assemblies first, then signs.  
    <img src="/images/image61.png" alt="Filter sign assemblies and signs" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 5: Fly to Assembly">
    Click the crosshair icon (“Fly to object”) to locate the first assembly.  
    <img src="/images/image62.png" alt="Fly to object icon" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 6: Lock View">
    Click the lock icon or press “L” to lock the camera on the assembly.  
    <img src="/images/image63.png" alt="Lock camera icon" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 7: Fill Assembly Attributes">
    Enter subattribute information (support type, count, condition, comments).  
    <img src="/images/image64.png" alt="Fill subattribute information" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 8: Review Signs">
    For each sign, press “C” to view imagery and complete subattributes.  
    <img src="/images/image65.png" alt="Review sign imagery" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 9: Approve or Delete">
    Approve (green) or delete (red) each sign based on accuracy.  
    <img src="/images/image66.png" alt="Approve or delete sign" />
  </StepperItem>
  <StepperItem title="Step 10: Proceed to Next">
    Click back on the first approved assembly, then use crosshair or “+” to move to the next.  
    <img src="/images/image67.png" alt="Navigate to next assembly" />
  </StepperItem>
</Stepper>
